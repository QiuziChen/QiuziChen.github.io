I am currently a graduate student at [TreesLab](https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/vp9b0jNsEdg8Pwyi09X_FA) led by Prof. [Ran Tu (涂然)](https://scholar.google.cz/citations?user=ueR4KsUAAAAJ&hl=zh-CN&oi=ao) in school of transportation, [Southeast University](https://www.seu.edu.cn/english/main.htm) (SEU).

I have participated in projects covering vehicle detection and tracking, public transit operation, accident risk modeling, and traffic emission modeling. These experiences gave me a broad interest in transportation issues and a belief that data and smart technologies could help us better solve these problems. At the same time, observations of my surroundings got me interested in issues of efficiency, equity and sustainability in transportation systems. For example, the confrontation between micro-mobility and cars, the prospects for air transportation in mountainous regions, the differences in accessibility and equity between urban and rural areas... I love all interesting research that addresses real problems, whether economic, policy, or technical, in a solid and innovative way.

My long-term goal is to help reshape cities to achieve **efficiency**, **equity** and **sustainability** by transportation management and operation. Currently, my research interets lie in the following topics:

- modeling of vehicular non-exhaust emissions
- causal inference and discovery
- multimodality and micromobility for urban transition
